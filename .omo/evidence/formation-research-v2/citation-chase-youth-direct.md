# Citation Chase: Youth/Adolescent and Middle-Distance Direct Evidence

- Search date: 2026-07-17 (Asia/Seoul)
- Canonical source ledger inspected: `reports/research/evidence/FORMATION_SOURCE_LEDGER.csv` (167 rows at search start)
- Scope: youth/adolescent runners, track-and-field athletes, and middle-distance runners; recovery mechanics, session composition, monitoring, safety boundaries, and measurement directness
- Product boundary: TRAINORACLE's 9.5-day Formation identity is fixed. This search does **not** decide adoption. It asks what the mechanics may measure, explain, defer, or stop.
- Evidence boundary: only primary articles, official bibliographic records, publisher pages, and institutional repositories were used as evidence. Search-result snippets were treated as discovery leads and verified against one of those source types where possible.
- Ledger boundary: no canonical source, search, screening, exclusion, extraction, or claim ledger was edited in this lane.

## Bottom line

Two backward and two forward citation-chasing rounds found additional direct youth/middle-distance studies. They improve three areas: (1) adolescent 800/1500-m intensity measurement, (2) within-session fatigue mechanics, and (3) prospective daily monitoring and injury observation. They do **not** supply a direct comparison of 7 versus 9.5 days, a validated 72-hour clearance rule, a youth middle-distance session-order/separation rule, or an automated safety threshold.

The most important new counterweight is prospective high-school cross-country evidence in which week-to-week sleep, sleep-quality, and fatigue changes were not associated with subsequent injury. This argues against promoting a single daily self-report to injury/readiness authority. The most important new mechanics evidence shows that high-intensity running to exhaustion changes plantar-flexor fatigue resistance, plantar loading, and leg-spring behavior in adolescent middle-distance runners; it does not say when the athlete is recovered enough for another MAIN session.

## Seed identification

### Directness rule

A ledger row was a seed when the sampled population or the studied event directly included one of the following:

1. youth/adolescent/young runners or endurance runners;
2. adolescent track-and-field/athletics athletes with an identifiable endurance subgroup;
3. 800-m, 1500-m, or explicitly middle-distance runners of any age;
4. a direct youth-runner safety, injury, monitoring, or measurement cohort.

General youth-sport, soccer, swimming, wearable, clinical-consensus, and adult distance-only rows were not called direct seeds even when they remained useful indirect context. Reviews and consensus documents with the exact target population were retained as citation-map/context seeds, not as primary-effect evidence.

## Claim impact

### Direct evidence added?

**Yes, as screening candidates.** Direct adolescent middle-distance/runner sources were found for physiological intensity description, within-session fatigue mechanics, prospective monitoring, and descriptive injury context. They have not been added to canonical ledgers by this lane.

### Contradiction-changing evidence added?

**No source reverses the current core claims.** The following refinements matter:

1. YC-13 is counterevidence against treating weekly changes in sleep, sleep quality, or fatigue as a validated injury/readiness predictor in high-school cross-country runners.
2. YC-07/YC-08 show clear within-session fatigue changes in adolescent middle-distance runners, but provide no recovery duration. They support representing fatigue as component-specific rather than a single color or score.
3. YC-10 shows that selected cardiac biomarkers may remain above baseline at 24 h and vary widely between adolescent runners. It does not prove incomplete performance recovery and must not become a medical readiness input.
4. YC-03/YC-04/YC-01 improve event- and age-direct intensity description, but none validates automated dose selection from lactate, VO2, or running-economy values alone.
5. YC-11 reports no apparent deterioration of antioxidant defense across a one-year professional-training observation, but does not establish program safety, session spacing, or causal benefit.

### Product-rule implications that are supportable now

- 9.5-day Formation may remain the fixed product identity and future default automated prescription target.
- Activation still requires deterministic eligibility, fail-closed exclusions, privacy, and human/coach gates.
- A nominal 72-hour MAIN target must remain an interval target, never clearance.
- Daily sleep, stress, fatigue, sRPE, biomechanics, biomarkers, and recent performance must remain separately named observations with method/time/provenance. None alone authorizes MAIN.
- Adolescent high-intensity fatigue is multi-component. Do not collapse plantar-flexor fatigue, running mechanics, subjective fatigue, and performance into a single inferred “neural fatigue” value.
- The product must not request or interpret cardiac/renal/oxidative biomarkers as routine athlete-entered readiness data.

## Access failures and bias

1. Direct `open` requests to multiple PubMed/PMC pages intermittently returned reCAPTCHA or timeout pages. Exact-title search results and official PubMed/PMC records were used when accessible; failures are not evidence of absence.
2. Scopus and Web of Science cited-by graphs were not available. Institutional pages sometimes displayed citation counts, but counts were not used to infer eligibility or completeness.
3. Google Scholar forward-citation graphs were not relied on. Search-engine discovery cannot reproduce a complete cited-by set.
4. Several publisher full texts were paywalled. Abstracts and institutional accepted manuscripts were used; methods/overlap judgments remain provisional where full text was unavailable.
5. SPORTDiscus and authenticated database searches were unavailable in this lane.
6. English-language/indexed-web bias remains. Korean, Chinese, Japanese, and coaching literature may be under-retrieved even though exact English DOI/title searches were used.
7. Very recent 2025-2026 sources have short forward-citation windows, so apparent lack of descendants is uninformative.

## Saturation judgment

**Status: QUERY-SATURATED WITHIN THE ACCESSIBLE WEB LANE; NOT SYSTEMATICALLY SATURATED.**

By the end of Round 2, exact-title, DOI, author-population, and PubMed/PMC/publisher searches repeatedly returned (a) already-ledger sources, (b) the same small adolescent middle-distance cohorts, or (c) age-/mode-indirect sources. This is sufficient to say that no accessible direct study was found for 7-vs-9.5-day comparison, 9.5-day efficacy/safety, universal 72-hour clearance, or youth middle-distance session-order/separation.

It is not sufficient to claim a complete systematic citation network. A human/librarian pass with SPORTDiscus, Scopus, Web of Science, and full-text cited-reference access is still required before formal evidence acceptance. The honest terminal state for this lane is therefore:

- direct mechanics candidates found: **yes**;
- direct safety/monitoring candidates found: **yes**;
- direct automatic-prescription evidence found: **no**;
- contradiction that invalidates fixed product identity found: **no**;
- evidence that permits runtime activation without the named gates found: **no**;
- human confirmation required: **yes**.
