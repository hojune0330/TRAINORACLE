## Source Contradictions And False Leads

1. [Runner's World 9-day overview](https://www.runnersworld.com/training/a44556541/9-day-training-cycle/)
   says Emma Bates used a 9-day cycle, but its linked Bates interview explicitly says 10-day.
   Mark this reuse `OVERSTATED` and do not count it as an independent 9-day case.
2. Search results attached a “n=9 well-trained runners undertaking a 10-day training cycle”
   snippet to DOI [`10.2147/OAJSM.S41655`](https://doi.org/10.2147/OAJSM.S41655).
   Crossref identifies that DOI as the 2014 review *Whole-body cryotherapy: empirical evidence
   and theoretical perspectives*. The snippet is contextual/reference text, not a direct
   9/10-day frame comparison. Exclude it from RQ-A frame evidence.
3. [Individualized endurance training trial](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35975912/)
   randomized 30 recreational runners to predefined or twice-weekly recovery/status-adjusted
   training. It supports the narrower proposition that deterministic individualized adjustment
   can be studied prospectively. It does not test 9.5 days, adolescents, or the TRAINORACLE
   architecture and cannot authorize its thresholds.
