## 1. Decision Answer

There is no primary trial in adolescent middle-distance runners that directly compares all of
the following:

1. endurance before strength versus strength before endurance;
2. same-session versus same-day separated versus adjacent-day delivery;
3. recovery or adverse-event outcomes through the next main exposure; and
4. a 9.5-day Formation against another cycle.

The strongest direct youth-running evidence supports only narrower statements:

- trained male adolescent 800-3000 m runners completed heavy resistance followed by matched
  plyometrics with 3-4 min intra-complex recovery, while maintaining endurance training;
- postpubertal adolescent distance runners completed plyometric/sprint work before resistance
  work within twice-weekly strength sessions separated by 2-4 days;
- six depth jumps followed by 10 min passive recovery acutely improved running economy, but not
  time to exhaustion, in male junior middle-distance runners; and
- replacing part of endurance work with explosive work can improve selected neuromuscular and
  anaerobic outcomes without establishing a preferred weekly order.

These studies do not establish one universal sequence, a universal number of hours between
components, a 48 h or 72 h clearance rule, or the safety of an automated composite session.
Recovery indicators also disagree: subjective readiness may normalize while contractile
function remains impaired.

The product consequence is not to abandon the fixed 9.5-day identity. It is to represent every
composite session as ordered, separately dosed components inside that identity, keep unknown
separation and recovery states unknown, and require human confirmation before runtime
prescription.

## 2. Scope, Method, And Directness Rules

### 2.1 Review question

For adolescent middle-distance runners, what primary evidence supports the order, interval,
and recovery treatment of endurance, resistance, plyometric, sprint/hill, and alternative-
aerobic components?

### 2.2 Search and trace method

The exact PubMed search strings and hit counts are preserved in
`search-rq-c.md`. The search prioritized adolescent/junior middle-distance runners, expanded to
youth athletes for sequence and recovery evidence, and then to trained adult runners or adult
concurrent-training trials for unresolved scheduling questions. Systematic reviews were used
as citation maps only. Product conclusions below were checked against the original trial,
PubMed record, PMC text, official publisher page, or an institutional accepted manuscript.

PubMed, PMC, DOI landing pages, official publisher pages, and institutional repositories were
checked through 2026-07-17. SPORTDiscus, Scopus, Web of Science, and Cochrane were not directly
searched because authenticated access was unavailable. This is therefore a deep targeted
primary-source review, not a PRISMA-complete systematic review.

### 3.1 What these trials permit

- A candidate library may contain the two observed order patterns as named templates:
  `HEAVY_RESISTANCE_THEN_MATCHED_PLYOMETRIC` and
  `PLYOMETRIC_SPRINT_THEN_RESISTANCE_WITHIN_STRENGTH_SESSION`.
- The template must retain its population, dose, source, and outcome purpose. It must not be
  presented as the universally correct order.
- Replacing endurance volume and adding a new component are different operations. The product
  must record `SUBSTITUTED`, `ADDED`, or `UNKNOWN`, not infer one from the other.

### 3.2 What these trials do not permit

- choosing endurance-first or strength-first for every adolescent;
- treating `>=48 h`, `2-4 days`, `10 min`, or `3-4 min` as interchangeable recovery rules;
- saying that an athlete is recovered because the study interval elapsed;
- converting absence of reported adverse events into a safety claim; or
- claiming that the 9.5-day architecture was validated by these interventions.

### 4.1 Recovery interpretation

The two youth-soccer recovery studies point in different directions because protocol, training
history, concurrent exposures, and measured outcomes differ. This is not a contradiction to be
averaged into `72 h`. It is evidence that recovery is component-, person-, context-, and
outcome-specific.

`perceived_ready`, `cmj_recovered`, `contractile_function_recovered`, and
`medical_clearance` are not synonyms. TRAINORACLE must not merge them into one green state.

### 5.1 Why review subgroup cutoffs are not product thresholds

The adult concurrent-training meta-analysis by Schumann et al. grouped studies as same-session
with <=20 min separation versus separated by >=3 h. Those are review bins across heterogeneous
studies, not a randomized validation that 3 h is sufficient for an adolescent runner. The
sequence review by Eddens et al. also found outcome-specific patterns rather than one order that
maximizes endurance, strength, power, and recovery simultaneously. These reviews were used to
trace original trials; they do not override the directness limits above.

## 6. Answers By Product Question

### Q1. Is there one best order?

**No.** Direct youth-running trials did not compare endurance-first with strength-first.
Observed successful protocols include heavy resistance before matched plyometrics,
plyometric/sprint before resistance within a strength session, and an acute plyometric primer
before running. Their goals differ. A sequence must therefore be attached to its intended
outcome and source, not promoted to a universal rule.

### Q2. Same session, same day, or adjacent day?

**No target-population head-to-head answer exists.** Same-session protocols can be compatible
in particular contexts, and adult evidence suggests immediate pairing can affect explosive or
strength outcomes. Direct adolescent runner studies do not validate a universal same-day hour
count or adjacent-day rule.

Permitted product treatment:

| Relation | Required representation | Automatic meaning prohibited |
|---|---|---|
| Same session | explicit component order and intra-session recovery | `optimal`, `safe`, or `recovered` |
| Same day, separated | actual/selected separation minutes; `UNKNOWN` if absent | minimum sufficient hours |
| Adjacent day | calendar relation plus prior component load state | recovery complete because date changed |
| Later in 9.5-day frame | actual elapsed time and intervening loads | frame position alone proves readiness |

### Q3. Does 72 h mean recovery is complete?

**No.** One youth-soccer protocol reported measures approaching baseline around 72 h, while an
ecological mixed-training study found maximal force and twitch function still impaired at 72 h.
Subjective wellness normalized earlier than contractile function. A clock is context, not
clearance.

### Q4. Is alternative aerobic work recovery?

It depends on the prescribed dose and purpose, and the available evidence is indirect.

| Candidate type | Product meaning | Load treatment |
|---|---|---|
| `LOW_INTENSITY_ACTIVE_RECOVERY` | A deliberately easy, versioned bout intended to support recovery | Still an exercise component; store modality, duration, intensity target, actual response, and provenance |
| `ALTERNATIVE_AEROBIC_TRAINING` | A fitness-maintenance/development session using cycling, pool, elliptical, etc. | Separate training load; never silently subtract from total load |
| `CYCLING_HIIT` | High-intensity conditioning by cycling | Separate high-load component; not recovery |
| `UNSPECIFIED_CROSS_TRAINING` | Purpose or dose missing | `UNKNOWN`; cannot receive a recovery label or automatic placement |

No identified primary trial validates cycling as universally lower-fatigue recovery for an
adolescent middle-distance runner. Reduced impact is not equivalent to reduced cardiovascular,
metabolic, or neuromuscular load.

## 8. User-Centered Explanation And Display

### 8.1 One-sentence explanation for a middle-school athlete

> 한 번에 여러 훈련을 할 때는 무엇을 먼저 했는지와 얼마나 쉬었는지를 따로 기록해요. 모든 선수에게 똑같이 맞는 순서는 아직 없어서, 코치가 오늘 목표와 네 반응을 보고 확인합니다.

### 8.2 Alternative-aerobic explanation

> 자전거나 수영을 회복 목적으로 가볍게 할 수는 있지만, 그것도 몸이 한 운동이에요. 세기와 시간을 모르면 앱이 자동으로 '회복 훈련'이라고 정하지 않아요.

### 8.3 Recovery explanation

> 72시간이 지났다는 사실만으로 회복 완료라고 표시하지 않아요. 몸의 느낌은 좋아져도 힘이나 점프 반응은 아직 덜 돌아올 수 있어요.

### 8.4 Recommended athlete-facing structure

Each composite session should show a simple ordered rail instead of one mixed block:

```text
[1 달리기: 주훈련] -- 같은 날 4시간 -- [2 웨이트: 힘] -- 4분 -- [3 플라이오: 반응]
                         코치 확인 필요
```

The first view should expose only:

- component name and goal;
- `같은 세션`, `같은 날`, or `다음 날`;
- recorded separation when known;
- `확인됨`, `정보 부족`, or `코치 확인 필요`; and
- the athlete's explicitly entered whole-session response.

Evidence grade, provenance, component dose, and competing observations belong in a detail view.
Do not use green to mean medically or physiologically recovered. A neutral check can mean only
`기록 완료` or `코치 확인 완료`, with the label always visible.

### 8.5 Useful progress without false certainty

- Give a daily check or sticker for completing a record, not for obeying an unverified plan or
  reaching a fatigue target.
- A streak means `기록을 이어 온 날`, not `안전하게 훈련한 날`.
- Show a small formation map so athletes know where they are in the 9.5-day identity, while a
  separate label says whether the next session is coach-confirmed.
- When data conflict, say `몸의 느낌과 힘 반응이 서로 달라요. 코치가 확인합니다.`

## 9. Automation Prohibition Lines

Until direct evidence and named human gates support a narrower contract, automated generation
must not:

1. prescribe strength-first, endurance-first, or plyometric-first as a universal youth rule;
2. treat 3 h, 6 h, 24 h, 48 h, 72 h, or any frame position as automatic clearance;
3. label a session safe because no adverse event was reported in a small trial;
4. infer recovery completion from one subjective, wearable, jump, or force measure;
5. call cycling, pool running, elliptical work, or jogging recovery when dose/purpose is missing;
6. replace a planned run with alternative aerobic work without coach-authorized equivalence;
7. collapse a composite session into a universal fatigue number;
8. proportionally split a whole-session sRPE among its components;
9. use ACWR, a wearable readiness score, or a threshold color as a plan-action rule;
10. infer health, illness, injury, or medical status, or choose a sequence from such a signal;
11. use private self-only memo content or metadata as any direct or indirect signal;
12. auto-catch-up, compress, or stack a missed main exposure into the rest of the 9.5-day frame;
13. claim scientific superiority or safety of 9.5 days from the cited component trials; or
14. activate runtime prescription before research, privacy, safety, data-quality, and human
    confirmation gates named in the canonical Formation contracts pass.

Eligible fail-closed output:

NO_AUTOMATED_PLAN_KEEP_CURRENT_COACH_AUTHORED_PLAN

## 10. Improvement Proposals For The Next Spec Pass

These are proposals for human review, not approved requirements.

1. **Separate record completion from readiness.** The current user experience can reward a
   completed diary/check-in while keeping recovery and plan authority neutral.
2. **Make composite order visible by default.** A single color or single fatigue block hides the
   exact issue the evidence says matters. Use an ordered rail, with fatigue detail as a secondary
   view.
3. **Ask one low-burden purpose question.** For alternative aerobic work, ask `회복 / 체력 유지 /
   고강도 훈련 / 잘 모르겠음`. Do not infer the answer from modality.
4. **Record actual separation automatically when timestamps are explicit.** Mark it `DERIVED`
   and preserve the underlying timestamps; never invent it for legacy records.
5. **Display uncertainty in ordinary language.** Replace technical warnings with `이 순서가
   모든 선수에게 맞는지는 아직 확인되지 않았어요.`
6. **Create a coach collision view.** If a main session, strength component, competition, or
   unresolved recovery observation collides, show all contributors and require one explicit
   coach decision. Do not silently move the main exposure.
7. **Pilot observability before efficacy.** A first shadow/candidate pilot can test whether
   athletes understand and complete component order, interval, and purpose records. It cannot
   establish safety or performance benefit.

## 12. Final Evidence Boundary

The evidence supports representing and auditing composite work, not silently simplifying it.
Within the fixed 9.5-day Formation identity, a candidate plan may organize endurance,
resistance, plyometric, hill/sprint, and alternative-aerobic components only when their purpose,
order, dose, interval, provenance, and human authority are explicit. It must not infer that
recovery is complete, that one sequence is universally superior, or that a small completed
trial proves safety.

This artifact remains a non-canonical candidate report until a human owner confirms its use in
the next specification pass.
